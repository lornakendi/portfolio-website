About Me

Analytical thinker with a foundation in applied statistics and a focus on real-world impact.

I’m Lorna Kendi, a junior data scientist with a background in Applied Statistics with Computing from Moi University. My path into data science was not accidental — it was a natural extension of how I think.

I’ve always been drawn to understanding patterns, structure, and logic. Studying statistics gave me the theoretical foundation, but I wanted more than equations — I wanted to apply analytical thinking to real-world problems. Data science allows me to transform curiosity into insight and insight into practical value.

Technically, I’m strongest in Python for data analysis and in statistical reasoning. I’m comfortable cleaning messy datasets, exploring patterns, and asking the right questions before jumping into solutions. I also have working knowledge of SQL, Excel, Power BI, and machine learning — and I’m continuously building depth in these areas.

I’m particularly drawn to real-world problems — economic trends, social issues, and complex systems that require both logic and strategy. My goal over the next few years is to grow into a data professional who operates not just technically, but strategically — creating meaningful impact while expanding globally.
